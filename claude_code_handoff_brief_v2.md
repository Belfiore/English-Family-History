## 🚨 NEW DISCOVERIES FROM THE WEDDING ALBUM
### (These update earlier versions of this brief — use these facts)

The wedding album (IMG_8831–IMG_8838) contains a formal inscription that reveals previously unknown facts:

- **Patricia's full name:** Patricia Mary FitzMaurice (later Andrews)
- **Her father Reg FitzMaurice** was still alive in February 1950 and lived at **Hayes, Middlesex**
- **Reg's wife (Patricia's mother)** is referred to as "the late Mrs. R. FitzMaurice" — she had already died before the wedding in 1950
- **Ronald's parents' initials:** Mr. and Mrs. **H. G. Andrews** of **East Ham, London** — both described as "the late", confirming both were dead before 1950. The East Ham address strongly suggests the Blitz bombing that killed them occurred in **East Ham, London**
- **Wedding date confirmed:** Saturday, 11th February 1950, at **St. Mary's Church, Wanstead**
- **Auntie Babs attended the wedding** — she is named in the group photo caption, confirming she was alive and in England in February 1950 (not permanently in South Africa at that time, or had returned to visit)
- The Korea newspaper clipping confirms the 7th Earl died **aged 84** — the clipping says he was "54 years married" to Connie — note: 54 years not 53 as previously stated. Small correction.

## THE NEW PAGE: FAMILY TREE

### Layout
- **LEFT COLUMN (~65% width):** Scrollable tree. Scrolls DOWN through time, oldest at top, present at bottom.
- **RIGHT COLUMN (~35% width):** Fixed biography + photo panel. NEVER moves when tree scrolls. When a person card is clicked, their bio and photos appear here instantly.

**CRITICAL:** Right panel must be independently scrollable but positionally fixed — use `position: sticky`, a flex layout with `overflow: hidden` on the parent and `overflow-y: auto` on each column, or equivalent. The user must never need to scroll up to see the bio after clicking a card at the bottom of the tree.

### Photo display in bio panel
Show photos ABOVE the biography text. Thumbnails in a horizontal row (max 120px tall), clickable to open a full-screen lightbox with caption. If a person has no photos, the photo section does not appear.
